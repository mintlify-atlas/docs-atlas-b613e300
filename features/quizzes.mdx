# Quizzes

Quizzes in StudyQuest provide rigorous knowledge testing through carefully crafted multiple-choice questions. Unlike flashcards that test recall, quizzes assess your deeper understanding with credible distractors and detailed explanations.

## How Quizzes Work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter a Quiz Level">
    Select a quiz-type level from your level map (indicated by the quiz icon). Each quiz contains 2-4 questions about a specific topic.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Read the Question">
    Questions are displayed prominently with a speaker icon. Tap the speaker to hear the question read aloud.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Your Answer">
    Four options are presented (A, B, C, D). Tap your chosen answer. You have 3 lives (hearts) - each wrong answer costs one life.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Receive Instant Feedback">
    - **Correct**: Green highlight, success sound, and celebratory avatar animation
    - **Incorrect**: Red highlight on your choice, green highlight on correct answer, wrong sound effect
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Read the Explanation">
    A detailed explanation appears explaining why the correct answer is right. This reinforces learning even when you answer incorrectly.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Continue or Game Over">
    - **With lives remaining**: Tap "Siguiente" to continue to the next question
    - **No lives left**: Game over dialog appears - you can retry the quiz later
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete the Quiz">
    Answer all questions correctly to complete the quiz, earn +20 XP, and unlock the next level.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Quiz Structure

### Question Format

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Question Display">
    - Clear, readable text in a white container
    - Volume button for text-to-speech
    - Question counter ("Pregunta 1 / 4")
    - Progress bar at the top of the screen
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Answer Options">
    - Four multiple-choice options (A, B, C, D)
    - Circular letter badges for easy identification
    - Tappable cards with hover feedback
    - Color-coded after selection (green = correct, red = incorrect)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Explanations">
    - Displayed after every answer attempt
    - Explains why the correct answer is right
    - Scrollable if explanation is lengthy
    - Color-matched to result (green box for correct, red for incorrect)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Lives System

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Three Hearts" icon="heart">
    You start each quiz with 3 lives, displayed as hearts in the top-right corner.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Wrong Answers" icon="heart-crack">
    Each incorrect answer costs one life. Hearts turn from filled to outlined.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Game Over" icon="heart-broken">
    Losing all 3 lives ends the quiz attempt. You must start over from the beginning.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Correct Answers" icon="heart-pulse">
    Correct answers don't restore lives - they preserve your remaining hearts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  The lives system adds pressure and encourages careful thinking before answering. Take your time to read each option thoroughly.
</Warning>

## Visual and Audio Feedback

### Success Indicators

**When you answer correctly**:
- Green highlight on the correct option
- Success sound effect ("correct.mp3")
- Animated avatar appears with celebration reaction
- Confetti animation (on quiz completion)
- Green feedback box with checkmark

### Error Indicators

**When you answer incorrectly**:
- Red highlight on your selected wrong answer
- Green highlight reveals the correct answer
- Error sound effect ("wrong.mp3")
- Animated avatar appears with sad/fail reaction
- Red feedback box with error icon
- One heart disappears from lives counter

<Note>
  Avatar animations appear as a popup in the center of the screen for 1.5 seconds with smooth scale and opacity transitions.
</Note>

## Question Quality

The AI generates quiz questions following strict guidelines:

<Accordion title="Credible Distractors">
  Wrong answers aren't obviously wrong. They're plausible alternatives that test real understanding rather than simple memorization. This forces deeper thinking about concepts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Analytical Questions">
  Questions test application and analysis, not just recall. Rather than "What is X?", questions ask "Why is X important?" or "When would you use X?"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Clear Explanations">
  Every question includes a detailed explanation that provides context, clarifies misconceptions, and reinforces the correct concept.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Balanced Difficulty">
  Questions range from foundational to advanced within each topic, scaffolding your learning progression.
</Accordion>

## Completion and Rewards

### Successful Completion

When you answer all quiz questions correctly (with at least 1 life remaining):

1. **Confetti Celebration**: Explosive confetti animation fills the screen
2. **Celebratory Dialog**: "¡Examen Completado! 🎓" with animated avatar
3. **XP Award**: +20 XP (double the flashcard reward)
4. **Level Complete**: Topic marked as completed
5. **Progress Saved**: Completion saved to your profile
6. **Next Level Unlocked**: Subsequent levels become available

### Game Over

If you lose all 3 lives:

1. **Game Over Dialog**: "¡Te quedaste sin vidas! 💔"
2. **Fail Avatar Animation**: Avatar shows disappointed reaction
3. **Encouragement Message**: "No te desanimes. Repasa los apuntes y vuelve a intentarlo."
4. **Return to Map**: Button to exit and review materials
5. **No XP Earned**: Must complete successfully to earn rewards

<Tip>
  If you fail a quiz, review the related flashcards first. The repetition helps solidify concepts before attempting the quiz again.
</Tip>

## Quiz vs Flashcards

| Feature | Flashcards | Quizzes |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Format** | Question/Answer flip | Multiple choice |
| **Lives** | Unlimited reviews | 3 lives (hearts) |
| **XP Reward** | +10 XP | +20 XP |
| **Difficulty** | Recall-based | Analysis-based |
| **Feedback** | Self-assessed | Immediate validation |
| **Learning Style** | Memorization | Comprehension testing |
| **Retry System** | Review loop in session | Full restart on game over |

<Note>
  Use flashcards to build foundational knowledge, then use quizzes to test and solidify understanding.
</Note>

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="When should I take quizzes?">
  Take quizzes after completing related flashcard levels. This ensures you have baseline knowledge before testing deeper understanding.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How many times can I retry a failed quiz?">
  Unlimited retries. Quiz progress resets on game over, allowing you to attempt as many times as needed to master the material.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do wrong answers affect my overall progress?">
  Wrong answers only affect the current quiz attempt (via the lives system). They don't impact your profile, XP, or level progression negatively.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I skip questions?">
  No. Questions must be answered in sequence. This ensures comprehensive topic coverage and prevents selective learning.
</Accordion>
