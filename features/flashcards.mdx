# Flashcards

Flashcards in StudyQuest provide an engaging, interactive way to memorize key concepts using proven spaced repetition techniques. Each flashcard presents a question on the front and the answer on the back, helping you actively recall information.

## How Flashcards Work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a Flashcard Level">
    From the level map, tap any flashcard-type level (indicated by the flashcard icon). Each level contains 3-5 flashcards related to a specific topic.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View the Question">
    The flashcard appears with the question displayed prominently. Take a moment to think about your answer before flipping.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Flip the Card">
    Tap anywhere on the card to reveal the answer with a smooth 3D flip animation. The card rotates to show the back side.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Hear the Answer (Optional)">
    Text-to-speech automatically reads the answer aloud when you flip the card, reinforcing auditory learning.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Self-Assess">
    Two buttons appear:
    - **"Lo sé" (I know it)**: Removes the card from your review queue
    - **"Repasar" (Review)**: Moves the card to the end of the queue for another review
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Continue Learning">
    Work through all cards in the queue until you've mastered them all. The progress bar shows your advancement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The Learning Algorithm

StudyQuest uses a queue-based spaced repetition system:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Initial Queue">
    When you start a flashcard session, all cards for that topic are loaded into a queue. The counter shows "Restantes: X" (Remaining: X).
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Confident Response">
    When you mark a card as "Lo sé" (I know it), it's permanently removed from the current session queue. This card won't appear again in this session.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Needs Review">
    When you mark a card as "Repasar" (Review), it's moved to the back of the queue. You'll see it again later in the same session after reviewing other cards.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Completion">
    The session ends when all cards have been marked as "known." You earn +10 XP and the level is marked as completed.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Tips for Effective Learning

<Tip>
  **Think before flipping**: Challenge yourself to recall the answer before flipping the card. This active recall strengthens memory formation.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Be honest with self-assessment**: Only mark cards as "known" when you can confidently explain the concept. It's okay to review cards multiple times.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use audio reinforcement**: Listen to the text-to-speech pronunciation. Hearing the answer engages multiple learning pathways.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Study in short sessions**: Review flashcards in 5-10 minute bursts rather than marathon sessions. This improves retention.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Regular practice**: Return to completed flashcard levels periodically to reinforce long-term memory.
</Tip>

## Completion Rewards

When you complete all flashcards in a level:

1. **XP Award**: Earn +10 XP instantly
2. **Level Complete**: The topic is marked as completed with a checkmark
3. **Next Level Unlocked**: The next level in the sequence may unlock
4. **Progress Saved**: Your completion is permanently saved to your profile

<Note>
  Flashcard sessions can be paused and resumed. Your progress is saved automatically as you work through the queue.
</Note>

## Flashcard Structure

Each flashcard contains:

- **ID**: Unique identifier
- **Front Text**: The question or concept prompt
- **Back Text**: The answer or explanation
- **Topic Association**: Linked to a specific topic in your world
- **Mastery Level**: Tracks your familiarity (starts at level 1)

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="How many flashcards should I review per session?">
  StudyQuest organizes flashcards by topic, with 3-5 cards per session. Complete entire topics rather than studying individual cards in isolation. This maintains conceptual connections.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if a flashcard seems incorrect?">
  AI-generated content aims for high accuracy but may occasionally have errors. Mark the card as "Review" and make a mental note. Future updates will include content editing.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I disable text-to-speech?">
  Currently, TTS is enabled by default. You can lower your device volume if you prefer silent study. Per-feature audio controls are planned for future releases.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does mastery level work?">
  Each flashcard has a mastery level (1-5) that tracks your long-term retention. Currently, this increases as you successfully review cards. Future updates will use this for adaptive scheduling.
</Accordion>
