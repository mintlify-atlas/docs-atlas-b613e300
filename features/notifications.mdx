# Notifications

StudyQuest uses local push notifications to help you maintain daily study habits and protect your learning streak. Timely reminders ensure you never miss a day of progress.

## Notification System

StudyQuest implements **local notifications** (not cloud-based) using Flutter Local Notifications with timezone support. This means:

- Notifications scheduled directly on your device
- No internet connection required for delivery
- Privacy-friendly (no data sent to external servers)
- Precise timing based on your local timezone

<Note>
  Local notifications work offline but require the app to be installed. Uninstalling the app removes all scheduled notifications.
</Note>

## Streak Reminder Notifications

The primary notification type is the **daily streak reminder**, designed to prevent streak breaks.

### How Streak Reminders Work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Automatic Scheduling">
    When you first launch StudyQuest or complete a level, the app schedules a daily reminder notification.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Notification Timing">
    By default, reminders are scheduled for **8:00 PM (20:00)** in your local timezone.
    
    _Test Mode: Currently set to 10 seconds after app launch for development testing._
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Daily Recurrence">
    The notification recurs every 24 hours at the same time, ensuring consistent daily reminders.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Notification Content">
    - **Title**: "¡Tu racha está en peligro! 🔥" (Your streak is in danger!)
    - **Body**: "Aún no has estudiado hoy. Entra a StudyQuest y completa una lección rápida." (You haven't studied today. Open StudyQuest and complete a quick lesson.)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Action on Tap">
    Tapping the notification opens StudyQuest directly to your home page, ready to study.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Notification Behavior

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Android">
    **Notification Channel**: "Recordatorios de Racha" (Streak Reminders)
    
    **Properties**:
    - Importance: Max (appears as heads-up notification)
    - Priority: High (ensures visibility)
    - Sound: Default notification sound
    - Vibration: Standard pattern
    - LED Color: Teal (#009688, matching app theme)
    - Icon: App launcher icon
    - Exact Timing: Uses `exactAllowWhileIdle` mode to bypass battery optimization
    
    **Permissions**:
    - `POST_NOTIFICATIONS` (Android 13+)
    - `SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM` (for precise 8 PM timing)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="iOS">
    **Properties**:
    - Alert: Shown as banner and in notification center
    - Sound: Default iOS notification sound
    - Badge: App icon badge may increment
    
    **Permissions**:
    - Alert permission requested on first launch
    - Sound permission included
    - Badge permission included
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Notification Schedule

### Current Implementation

```dart
// Scheduled time (production)
var scheduledDate = tz.TZDateTime(
  tz.local, 
  now.year, 
  now.month, 
  now.day, 
  20  // 8:00 PM
);

// If already past 8 PM today, schedule for tomorrow
if (scheduledDate.isBefore(now)) {
  scheduledDate = scheduledDate.add(Duration(days: 1));
}

// Notification scheduled with exact timing
await notificationsPlugin.zonedSchedule(
  0,  // Notification ID
  '¡Tu racha está en peligro! 🔥',
  'Aún no has estudiado hoy...',
  scheduledDate,
  platformSpecifics,
  androidScheduleMode: AndroidScheduleMode.exactAllowWhileIdle
);
```

### Timezone Handling

Notifications respect your local timezone:

- **Timezone Detection**: Automatically uses device timezone
- **Current Setting**: America/Mexico_City (UTC-6/-5)
- **DST Support**: Automatically adjusts for daylight saving time
- **Travel-Friendly**: Notifications adjust if you change timezones

<Tip>
  If you travel across timezones, notifications will continue firing at 8 PM in the new timezone. Restart the app to reschedule for accurate local timing.
</Tip>

## Permission Handling

### Android Permissions

On Android 13+ (API level 33+), notification permissions are requested automatically:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initial Request">
    On first app launch, a system dialog asks: "Allow StudyQuest to send you notifications?"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="User Choice">
    - **Allow**: Notifications enabled immediately
    - **Deny**: Notifications won't appear (can be enabled later in system settings)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Exact Alarm Permission">
    A second permission for exact alarms may be requested to ensure precise 8 PM timing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### iOS Permissions

On iOS, permissions are requested during `NotificationService.init()`:

- Alert permission (show banners)
- Sound permission (play notification sounds)
- Badge permission (show app icon badge)

Users can grant or deny. Denied permissions can be changed in iOS Settings > StudyQuest > Notifications.

<Warning>
  If notifications aren't appearing, check your device's notification settings for StudyQuest. Ensure notifications are enabled and not silenced.
</Warning>

## Notification Management

### Canceling Notifications

Currently, notifications are canceled and rescheduled each time `scheduleStreakReminder()` is called:

```dart
await _notificationsPlugin.cancelAll();
// Then reschedule with new timing
```

This ensures only one active reminder exists, preventing notification spam.

### Disabling Notifications

To disable notifications:

1. **Via System Settings** (recommended):
   - Android: Settings > Apps > StudyQuest > Notifications > Toggle off
   - iOS: Settings > StudyQuest > Notifications > Toggle off

2. **Via App** (future feature):
   - In-app settings to disable streak reminders
   - Preserves ability to re-enable without system settings

<Note>
  There's currently no in-app toggle to disable notifications. This feature is planned for future releases.
</Note>

## Notification Best Practices

<Accordion title="When should I expect notifications?">
  You'll receive your first streak reminder at 8:00 PM the day after you complete a level. Subsequent reminders occur daily at 8 PM as long as the app is installed.

## Technical Implementation

### NotificationService Structure

```dart
class NotificationService {
  static final FlutterLocalNotificationsPlugin _notificationsPlugin;
  
  // Initialize with timezone and platform settings
  static Future<void> init() async {
    // Timezone setup
    tz.initializeTimeZones();
    tz.setLocalLocation(tz.getLocation('America/Mexico_City'));
    
    // Platform-specific settings
    const androidSettings = AndroidInitializationSettings('@mipmap/ic_launcher');
    const iosSettings = DarwinInitializationSettings(...);
    
    // Request permissions
    await _notificationsPlugin.initialize(...);
    await requestPermissions();
  }
  
  // Schedule daily streak reminder
  static Future<void> scheduleStreakReminder() async {
    await _notificationsPlugin.cancelAll();  // Clear old reminders
    
    // Calculate next 8 PM
    final scheduledDate = calculateNext8PM();
    
    // Schedule with exact timing
    await _notificationsPlugin.zonedSchedule(
      0,
      title,
      body,
      scheduledDate,
      platformSpecifics,
      androidScheduleMode: AndroidScheduleMode.exactAllowWhileIdle
    );
  }
}
```

### Calling the Service

Notifications are initialized at app startup:

```dart
void main() async {
  WidgetsFlutterBinding.ensureInitialized();
  
  // Initialize notification service
  await NotificationService.init();
  await NotificationService.scheduleStreakReminder();
  
  runApp(MyApp());
}
```

## Battery Optimization

Android's battery optimization can interfere with exact notification timing:

### AndroidScheduleMode.exactAllowWhileIdle

This mode ensures notifications fire even when the device is in low-power mode:

- Bypasses Doze mode restrictions
- Delivers at exact scheduled time (not deferred)
- Requires `SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM` permission

<Warning>
  Some Android manufacturers (Xiaomi, Huawei, OnePlus) have aggressive battery optimization. Users may need to whitelist StudyQuest in battery settings for reliable notifications.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Notifications not appearing">
  **Solutions**:
  1. Check app notification permissions in system settings
  2. Ensure "Do Not Disturb" mode isn't blocking notifications
  3. Whitelist StudyQuest in battery optimization settings
  4. Verify the app is installed (uninstalling removes scheduled notifications)
  5. Restart the app to reschedule notifications
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Notifications appear at wrong time">
  **Causes**:
  - Device timezone set incorrectly
  - Recent timezone change (travel) without app restart
  
  **Solution**: Verify device timezone and restart StudyQuest to reschedule.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Multiple duplicate notifications">
  **Cause**: Notification scheduling called multiple times without proper cancellation.
  
  **Solution**: This shouldn't happen in production. If it does, restart the app. The `cancelAll()` call should prevent duplicates.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No permission dialog appeared">
  **Android**: Check if you previously denied permissions. Go to Settings > Apps > StudyQuest > Permissions > Notifications and enable manually.
  
  **iOS**: Permissions may have been denied during initial launch. Go to Settings > StudyQuest > Notifications and enable.
</Accordion>
